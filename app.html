What Worked

  • Google Ads is the dominant channel ($50K of $66K, 76% of spend) — highest volume driver
  • Digital Marketing GenAI course has 37.6% CTR — strongest performer across all courses
  • Free talks (A*star, ASME) generate FOI at $0 CPL — scalable model
  • SME Centre Conference: 34 FOI at $96 CPL — best paid event ROI
  • Events overall drove significant pipeline (SI Events is a top lead source)

What Didn't Work

  • Meta Ads declined from $3.6K to near zero — likely poor ROAS causing budget cut
  • LinkedIn Ads inconsistent ($746 then $0 then $236 then $0) — no sustained strategy
  • DPTM webinar: $291 CPL with only 3 FOI — worst performing event
  • No attribution tracking: cannot tie ad spend to actual revenue closed
  • 14% of revenue from AI courses but 35% of deals are AI — pricing gap?

Recommendations

  • Kill Meta Ads or redesign creative (declining to zero = abandoned)
  • LinkedIn needs a 3-month test with consistent $500/mo budget
  • Add UTM tracking to connect ad spend to registration to deal close
  • Double down on free/low-cost talks (A*star model: 63 FOI at $0)
  • AIGP has low CTR (18%) despite strong demand — rework landing page

Current State: Spreadsheet Chaos

  • 17 separate sheets across one workbook
  • No deal sizes on most pipeline entries
  • No stage dates or deal velocity tracking
  • No connection between marketing spend and sales outcomes
  • Manual updates — data goes stale within days
  • No dashboard view — managers must read row by row
  • Pipeline values missing from most rep sheets
  • No way to identify stale deals (no last-updated date)
  • Lost deals tracked inconsistently across reps

Recommended: CRM-Light System

  • Single pipeline view with Kanban board (see below)
  • Every deal has a value, source, stage, and timestamp
  • Automated alerts for stale deals (>30 days no movement)
  • Marketing attribution: Ad to Registration to Lead to Deal to Close
  • Rep performance scorecards auto-generated weekly
  • Weekly auto-generated pipeline and revenue reports
  • UTM parameters on all campaigns connecting spend to revenue
  • Deal velocity metrics: time-in-stage, conversion rates
  • Market-level breakdowns (SG / MY / PH)
